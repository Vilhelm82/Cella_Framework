                                                 Abstract
      Both methods act on the same object: the Hessian of a thermodynamic fundamental relation
      Φ(E a ). GTD wraps that Hessian in a Legendre–covariant conformal factor and reads the cur-
      vature scalar of the resulting metric; its singularities mark the instability (Davies) locus. We
      show DBP admits a parallel construction — a metric assembled from the role–coupling channels,
      g DBP = −h−1 — whose curvature is likewise a single scalar, and that the two curvatures are
      complementary. On the Kerr black hole we verify in exact computer algebra that conventional
      GTD-II’s curvature has a double pole on the Davies transition and is provably blind to the ex-
      tremal boundary; the DBP curvature does the opposite, diverging on both physical boundaries
      and finite on Davies. This complementarity is universal: across Kerr (spin), Reissner–Nordström
      (charge), and the van der Waals fluid (no reflection symmetry, a genuine critical point), GTD-
      II’s curvature carries an order–2 pole on the instability locus while the DBP curvature carries an
      order–3 pole on each role boundary — order 4 where a boundary meets a reflection–symmetry
      fixed locus — the two singular sets disjoint. The DBP geometry is geodesically incomplete,
      its boundaries finite–distance curvature edges. At n = 3 (Kerr–Newman) a two–state demon-
      stration shows that both single scalars are lossy and the complete S3 –invariant is a faithful
      three–dimensional carrier. Transcribed literature and independently verified computation are
      distinguished throughout by inline certainty tiers.

5.2   DBP for Kerr–Newman: the faithful carrier
With (S, J, Q) the three roles and M the graph value, each of the three charge–output charts carries
its own coupling channel exactly as in §3, and the unordered triple is again an S3 –orbit. Evaluated
at a regular interior point (S, J, Q) = (5, 21 , 25 ) the three chart denominators are the three physical
response functions

S-output: g1 = T (Directive),       J-output: g2 = Ω (Substrate),         Q-output: g3 = Φe (Product),

and the extremal locus (25) is, once again for free, the role–chart singularity where the entropy–
output channels diverge as g1 = T → 0. The same boundary GTD-II’s R cannot see is a coordinate
singularity of the DBP construction. [symbolic]
    The new content at n = 3 is that the single scalar is no longer enough — and DBP already
carries the object that is. The complete S3 –invariant of the coupling is not Ac but the faithful three–
dimensional role–channel carrier O = (O12 , O13 , O23 ) (the off–diagonal RoleChSpec coordinates);
at the point above, O = (−0.162, −0.086, −0.856). [computer-verified]

Result 3 (The invariant content is a faithful 3–dimensional carrier; Ac is its lowest piece). The role–
channel map sends a coupling state to the orbit carrier O ∈ R3 , and this map is faithful: the 6 × 3
rank–one channel matrix has rank 3, with an explicit minor certificate 32/(g1 g2 g3 ) = 25 /(g1 g2 g3 )
in characteristic 6= 2 and a separate characteristic–2 certificate, so the carrier is 3–dimensional
in every characteristic; the faithfulness of the coupling carrier is established in the foundational
calculus [1]. The scalar Ac is the squared norm of the standard–irrep part of O — the lowest
non–trivial S3 –invariant. It is therefore a genuine but lossy summary: distinct carriers can share
an Ac value. This resolves what an earlier draft posed as an open “invariant ring” target — the
complete invariant content is the carrier itself, and Ac is one coordinate of it.

5.3   Two states, one R, one Ac , two carriers
The promotion above has teeth only if the loss is real. It is, and the demonstration is the centrepiece
of the n = 3 case. By pseudo–arclength continuation along the simultaneous level set {R =
const} ∩ {Ac = const} we located two physically distinct regular Kerr–Newman states

                 p1 = (S, J, Q) = (12, 0.6, 0.5),       p2 = (13.7965, 0.6954, 0.5589),

on which the two scalar diagnostics are each constant to machine precision:

          R(p1 ) = R(p2 ) = 269.3252,       Ac (p1 ) = Ac (p2 ) = 297.1150,     |∆| ≈ 10−13 ,       (29)

yet whose carriers differ, kO(p1 ) − O(p2 )k ≈ 0.047, and whose six measured rank–one channels
separate by 9%. [computer-verified] Both single–scalar diagnostics — GTD’s curvature R and DBP’s
own Ac — collapse a pair that the faithful three–dimensional carrier cleanly distinguishes.
Remark 1 (Where the honesty is). The contrast here is sharp but narrow, and it is worth stating
exactly where it bites. It is a statement about the two pipelines at the scalar diagnostic level —
the level at which each pipeline, as practised, actually terminates and reports a number. It is not
the claim that GTD’s full metric g II is degenerate on this pair (it is a nine–component object and
we have not shown it coincides), and it is pointedly not a claim that DBP is immune: Ac fails on
exactly the same pair. n = 3 is simply the first place where the single–scalar summary — whichever
pipeline produces it — is provably incomplete, and where DBP happens to retain the faithful object
one rung below its own headline scalar.

6.5   What remains open
Two directions are not closed by the above.
Open — the holonomy route (old Target 2b).
The maps s, t are transports between the three role charts; treated as a discrete connection,
their holonomy around a loop encircling a boundary is the monodromy of the re–solving
cover around its ramification locus (the role–boundary), which is the wreath machinery of the
monodromy companion. The earlier conjecture “holonomy = Ac ” cannot hold literally — Ac
is a continuous scalar, the monodromy a discrete group element — and the honest statement
is a bridge relating Ac ’s residue near a boundary to the cycle type of the monodromy there.
This is the same thread as the companion and is not pursued further here.

Open — the n = 3 lift.
The metric g DBP = −h−1 and the complementarity above are established for n = 2. At n = 3
(Kerr–Newman, §5) each output chart carries several off–diagonal couplings rather than one,
so the channel form h must be generalised before the construction transfers. Whether the
complementarity survives against the n = 3 Davies surface is the natural next test.

The one–sentence statement. GTD and DBP read the same Hessian: GTD builds a metric
whose curvature is singular on the instability locus and blind to the boundaries; DBP builds a
metric whose curvature is finite on the instability locus and singular on the boundaries — and
across spin, charge, and fluid these two singular sets are disjoint, with a universal order law (2 for
the transition, 3 for a boundary, 4 for a reflection–fixed boundary).
