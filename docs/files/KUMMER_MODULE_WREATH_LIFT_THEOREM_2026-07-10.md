# Conjugate Kummer Modules and Wreath-Product Closures

**Date:** 2026-07-10  
**Status:** General theorem, proof complete  
**Origin:** Abstraction of the R9 signed-contact valuation matrix  
**Purpose:** Separate a decorated cover into its base monodromy quotient and its conjugate Kummer kernel

## 1. Setup

Let `F` be a field of characteristic different from two. Let `K/F` be a finite separable extension of degree `d`, let `E/F` be its normal closure, and put

```text
G = Gal(E/F).
```

Throughout, `C_2^s` means the elementary abelian group `(C_2)^s`, and
`C_2^s wreath_I G` denotes the permutational wreath product for the action
of `G` on `I`.

Let

```text
I = Hom_F(K,E).
```

Then `|I|=d`, and `G` acts transitively and faithfully on `I`. Choose nonzero elements

```text
r_1,...,r_s in K^*.
```

For `i in I`, write

```text
r_{alpha,i} = i(r_alpha) in E^*,
1 <= alpha <= s.                              (1.1)
```

Define the decorated sheet field

```text
H = K(sqrt(r_1),...,sqrt(r_s))                (1.2)
```

and the conjugate radical composite

```text
L = E(
      sqrt(r_{alpha,i}) :
      1<=alpha<=s, i in I
    ).                                         (1.3)
```

## 2. The conjugate square-class module

Let

```text
V = F_2^s tensor_F2 F_2[I].                   (2.1)
```

It has basis vectors `e_{alpha,i}` indexed by the `sd` conjugate radicands. There is a natural `F_2[G]`-module map

```text
theta: V -> E^*/E^{*2},
theta(e_{alpha,i}) = [r_{alpha,i}].            (2.2)
```

Define

```text
R = ker(theta),
W = im(theta) = V/R.                          (2.3)
```

Thus:

```text
R = the module of multiplicative square relations among all conjugate
    radicands;

W = the conjugate Kummer module;

rho = dim_F2 W = sd-dim_F2 R.                 (2.4)
```

Both `R` and `W` are `G`-stable.

## 3. Normal-closure theorem

### Theorem 3.1

The field `L` in (1.3) is the normal closure of `H/F`. Moreover,

```text
Gal(L/E) ~= Hom_F2(W,F_2) ~= (C_2)^rho,        (3.1)
```

and restriction gives an exact sequence

```text
1 -> W^vee -> Gal(L/F) -> G -> 1.             (3.2)
```

#### Proof

Let `N` be the normal closure of `H/F`. Since `K subset H`, the field `N` contains the normal closure `E` of `K/F`. Every `F`-embedding of `H` restricts to an element `i in I` and sends

```text
sqrt(r_alpha) -> +/-sqrt(r_{alpha,i}).
```

Therefore `N` contains every generator of `L`, so `L subseteq N`.

Conversely, an `F`-embedding of `L` permutes the embeddings `i in I` through the action of `G` and sends every displayed square root to a signed square root of another displayed radicand. Hence it preserves `L`. Thus `L/F` is normal and separable and contains `H`, so `N subseteq L`. This proves equality.

Kummer theory over `E` identifies the Galois group generated by the square roots with the dual of the span of their square classes, giving (3.1). Since `L/F` and `E/F` are Galois, restriction is surjective and has kernel `Gal(L/E)`, proving (3.2). ∎

## 4. The valuation-rank criterion

Every discrete valuation `v` of `E` defines an `F_2`-linear functional

```text
lambda_v: W -> F_2,
lambda_v([x]) = v(x) mod 2.                   (4.1)
```

This is well-defined because every square has even valuation.

Choose discrete valuations

```text
v_1,...,v_m
```

of `E`. Their parity matrix on the conjugate radicands is

```text
A = ( v_q(r_{alpha,i}) mod 2 )
```

with `m` rows and `sd` columns.

### Theorem 4.1 — valuation separation

If

```text
rank_F2 A = sd,                                (4.2)
```

then

```text
R=0,
W~=V,
rho=sd.                                       (4.3)
```

#### Proof

A square-class relation is a vector `c in V` such that

```text
product_{alpha,i} r_{alpha,i}^{c_{alpha,i}}
```

is a square in `E`. Applying every valuation gives

```text
A c = 0.
```

Full column rank forces `c=0`. Therefore `theta` is injective and (4.3) follows. ∎

This criterion is one-directional in exactly the useful way: a full-rank valuation matrix proves maximality without requiring factorization, resolvents, or a census of finite permutation groups.

## 5. Orbit form of the criterion

The R9 pattern is more structured than an arbitrary full-rank matrix.

Suppose that for `q=1,...,s` there is a discrete valuation `v^(q)` of `E`, a distinguished sheet `i_q in I`, and a row vector

```text
b_q = (b_{q1},...,b_{qs}) in F_2^s
```

such that

```text
v^(q)(r_{alpha,j})
  = b_{q alpha} mod 2     when j=i_q,
  = 0 mod 2               when j!=i_q.        (5.1)
```

Let

```text
B = (b_{q alpha}) in M_s(F_2).                (5.2)
```

### Theorem 5.1 — orbit valuation matrix

If `B` is invertible, then all `sd` conjugate radicands are square-class independent.

#### Proof

For each `q` and each target sheet `i`, choose `g in G` with

```text
g(i_q)=i,
```

which is possible by transitivity. Transporting `v^(q)` by `g` gives a valuation whose parity row is supported on sheet `i` and equals `b_q` within that sheet.

After ordering rows by `q,i` and columns by `alpha,i`, the resulting `sd x sd` matrix is, up to simultaneous row and column permutation,

```text
B tensor I_d.                                  (5.3)
```

Its determinant is nonzero because `B` is invertible. Theorem 4.1 applies. ∎

### Geometric verification route

In applications, the valuations in (5.1) often arise from prime divisors on the observable parameter space. A sufficient verification package is:

1. the base divisor is not a component of the discriminant of the degree-`d` cover;
2. hence it is unramified in `E/F` at its generic point;
3. exactly one conjugate sheet has the required odd valuation;
4. all off-sheet radicands have even valuation; and
5. the relevant radicands are regular on a normal localized model.

The `G`-orbit of one prolongation then supplies the `d` rows associated with that divisor type.

## 6. The wreath-lift theorem

Choose square roots

```text
x_{alpha,i}=sqrt(r_{alpha,i}).
```

Every element `tau in Gal(L/F)` restricts to some `g in G` and acts by

```text
tau(x_{alpha,i})
  = epsilon_{alpha,i}(tau) x_{alpha,g(i)},

epsilon_{alpha,i}(tau) in {+1,-1}.            (6.1)
```

Thus, after the choices of square roots, there is a faithful signed-permutation representation

```text
Gal(L/F) -> (C_2^s)^I semidirect G.           (6.2)
```

### Theorem 6.1 — maximal conjugate Kummer lift

If the equivalent maximality conditions

```text
R=0,
rho=sd,
```

hold, then (6.2) is an isomorphism:

```text
Gal(L/F)
  ~= (C_2^s)^d semidirect G
   = C_2^s wreath_I G.                        (6.3)
```

In particular,

```text
|Gal(L/F)| = 2^(sd)|G|.                       (6.4)
```

#### Proof

The representation (6.2) is injective because an automorphism fixing `E` and every chosen square root fixes the generators of `L`.

By Theorem 3.1 and maximality,

```text
|Gal(L/F)|
  = |Gal(L/E)| |G|
  = 2^(sd)|G|.
```

The target of (6.2) has the same order. Hence the injection is surjective. ∎

### Corollary 6.2 — symmetric-base form

If the base monodromy is fully symmetric,

```text
G=S_d,
```

and the valuation criterion proves maximality, then

```text
Gal(L/F) ~= C_2^s wreath S_d.                 (6.5)
```

This is the form activated by the all-`k` symmetric-monodromy theorem.

## 7. Inertia interpretation

Assume a discrete valuation `v` of `E` has residue characteristic different from two, and pass to the henselization at `v`. Under the Kummer pairing

```text
Gal(L/E) x W -> F_2,
```

the valuation functional `lambda_v` in (4.1), viewed as an element of
`W^vee=Gal(L/E)`, is the nontrivial tame-inertia element at `v` whenever
`lambda_v` is nonzero.

More explicitly, write locally

```text
r_{alpha,i}
  = pi^{m_{alpha,i}} unit_{alpha,i}.
```

Then:

```text
all m_{alpha,i} even
  => the Kummer extension is unramified at v;

some m_{alpha,i} odd
  => the inertia subgroup has order two,
     and its nontrivial element sends

     sqrt(r_{alpha,i})
       -> (-1)^{m_{alpha,i}} sqrt(r_{alpha,i}). (7.1)
```

Thus each row of the valuation matrix has two simultaneous meanings:

```text
linear-algebra meaning: it detects square-class relations;
geometric meaning:      it records the local inertia sign pattern.
```

The inertia stratification and the Kummer-module calculation are therefore the same data viewed dually.

## 8. Relation modules and nonmaximal lifts

Full symmetric base monodromy does not by itself force a full wreath product. If `R!=0`, then `R` is a nonzero `F_2[G]`-submodule of

```text
F_2^s tensor F_2[I].
```

The Kummer kernel is

```text
(V/R)^vee,
```

and `Gal(L/F)` is the corresponding signed-permutation subgroup of the maximal wreath product.

Therefore every failure of maximality has a precise form:

```text
a G-stable family of multiplicative square relations among conjugate
radicands.                                     (8.1)
```

This is the correct falsifier for any proposed maximal wreath theorem.

## 9. Static four-charge crown

For the static crown,

```text
d=5,
G=S_5,
s=2,
r_1=u,
r_2=gamma.
```

The even and odd signed contact divisors give the sheet-level matrix

```text
B = [1 0]
    [1 1].                                    (9.1)
```

It is invertible over `F_2`. Theorem 5.1 gives rank ten, and Theorem 6.1 gives

```text
Gal(H_tilde/F)
  ~= C_2^2 wreath S_5,

|Gal(H_tilde/F)|
  = 2^10 * 120
  = 122880.                                   (9.2)
```

The original R9 proof is exactly this two-row instance of the general theorem.

## 10. Rotating three-channel template

For the augmented rotating cover, the anticipated three families are

```text
r_1=u,
r_2=gamma,
r_3=delta_J=gamma-4P-16J^2.
```

The valuation types are expected to have the matrix

```text
B_rot = [1 0 0]     even signed contact
        [1 1 0]     odd signed contact
        [0 0 1]     private delta_J prime.     (10.1)
```

Its determinant is one. Once the private primes are checked to be distinct across conjugate sheets, Theorem 5.1 gives rank `3d`. For the four-charge base `d=5`, this predicts

```text
Gal(L_rot/F(J)) ~= C_2^3 wreath S_5.           (10.2)
```

Equation (10.2) is an application target, not promoted by the present abstract theorem alone.

## 11. All-k interface

The all-`k` monodromy theorem supplies

```text
G=S_{delta_k}
```

for every `k>=3`. Therefore any `s`-channel decorated realization above the axial mass cover that supplies an invertible sheet-level valuation matrix has closure group

```text
C_2^s wreath S_{delta_k}.                     (11.1)
```

The work remaining in each realization is now sharply isolated:

```text
define the radicand families;
identify divisor types and their parity rows;
prove regularity and unramifiedness;
check that the sheet-level matrix is invertible.
```

The base permutation group no longer needs to be recomputed.

## 12. Proof-use checklist

Before applying the theorem to a new decorated family, verify:

```text
[ ] K/F finite separable of degree d.
[ ] E is the normal closure and G is known on the d embeddings.
[ ] Every radicand r_alpha is nonzero in K.
[ ] Conjugate radicands are regular at the chosen divisors.
[ ] Chosen base divisors survive in the normal model.
[ ] Ramification indices do not erase the required odd valuations.
[ ] Off-sheet valuations are even.
[ ] The sheet-level parity matrix B is invertible.
```

Only after these checks may the maximal wreath conclusion be promoted.

## 13. Bottom line

The normal closure of a decorated non-Galois sheet is governed by one `G`-module:

```text
W = span of all conjugate radicand square classes.
```

Valuation rows are elements of `W^vee`. If their `G`-orbits separate every conjugate generator, then `W` is the full permutation module, the Kummer kernel is maximal, and the normal-closure group is the exact wreath product

```text
C_2^s wreath G.
```

With the axial base now `S_{delta_k}` for every `k`, this theorem is the reusable engine for every Kummer-decorated lift above it.
