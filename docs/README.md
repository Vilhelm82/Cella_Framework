## Mathematical primitives and operations

The descriptions below identify the mathematical objects in each top-level item and the operations performed or specified there. A paper or report *states and proves* operations; a script or Macaulay2 file *executes* them; a raw output only records a particular run.

### `files/`

This is the broad working archive. Its mathematics falls into five connected families.

#### Active recharting and DBP curvature geometry

- **Primitives:** Constraint hypersurfaces, output-chart jets, active recharting orbits, Hessian gauge classes, labelled curvature channels, inverse-channel metrics, role divisors, local metric germs, and Newton corner supports.
- **Operations:** Takes gradients and Hessians of implicit constraints; quotients jets by active chart changes; decomposes bordered-Hessian/Gaussian curvature into channel accounts; selects a positive, interior-regular Kerr-Newman metric; and evaluates scalar-curvature asymptotics at `T=0`, `Omega=0`, and `Phi_e=0`.
- **Pole laws:** The generic quadratic face has order 3, while a parity-fixed inverse-channel face has `R ~ -m(m+5) x^-4/B`. At the double-reflection corner the path order is controlled by a Newton support maximum, including the recorded wedge `max(4a-2, 4-2a)`.
- **Proof methods:** Exact rational identities, coefficient-positivity after shifts, Sturm sequences, local Laurent expansions, and bordered-Hessian formulas.

Relevant artifacts include the unified theorem-spine source/PDF, the LEAD7 Kerr-Newman PDF, the local-curvature note, and the mathematics-first valuation plan.

#### Axial `k`-ellipse and mass-fiber algebra

- **Primitives:** The signed radical equation `4M = sum_i sqrt(u+N_i^2)`, its norm eliminant in `u=m^2`, axial symmetric pencils, critical/caustic loci, and finite mass-sheet covers.
- **Operations:** Eliminates the radicals by multiplying over sign choices; determines polynomial degree and leading coefficient; factors reductions modulo good primes; reads factor degrees as Frobenius cycle types; and uses those cycles plus transitivity to constrain or identify the Galois group.
- **Reality and selection:** Represents the strict physical chamber by definiteness of a symmetric pencil, factors the mass polynomial spectrally, proves all relevant roots real and positive, and identifies the physical root as the least root/first spectrahedral exit.
- **Generic geometry:** Uses incidence varieties, discriminants, resultants, Jacobian/nondegeneracy conditions, and parameter variation to prove generic simplicity and separation of caustic critical values.

Relevant artifacts include the all-`k` note/results, `K5_GALOIS_GROUP_CERTIFICATE_REPORT`, R5/R6/R7 logs and audits, and the evolving research maps.

#### Horizon covers, Kummer extensions, and wreath products

- **Primitives:** The Kerr-Newman quadratic horizon pair, the `Z/2` horizon-swap action, the four-charge quintic mass field, conjugate square classes in `L*/L*2`, valuation-parity matrices, Kummer extensions, inertia vectors, colored cycles, and wreath-product normal closures.
- **Quadratic operations:** Applies Vieta to `z^2-e1 z+e2`, splits horizon data into invariant/even and covariant/odd parts, and derives the entropy product and left/right combinations from the deck grading.
- **Abel-Ruffini operations:** Certifies an `S_5` mass-fiber specialization via Dedekind/Frobenius data, uses specialization to obstruct solvability of the generic fiber, and transfers the obstruction to the entropy sum through a resultant/minimal-polynomial argument while the entropy product remains rational.
- **Kummer operations:** Builds square-class modules generated by Galois conjugates, evaluates divisorial valuations modulo 2, proves independence by full-rank parity matrices or private-prime rows, and concludes normal closures of the form `C_2^r wr S_5`.
- **Inertia operations:** Associates each branch divisor with a parity row, lifts base permutations with Kummer colors, and organizes intersections into a realized branch-stratum poset.

Relevant artifacts include the degree-20 crown, Kummer lift, static/rotating closure, rank-jump, inertia-stratification, and R9 reports.

#### Research governance and audit

- **Primitives:** Candidate objects, theorem/evidence status, proof gates, loss matrices, source dependencies, promotion rulings, and stop-proof markers.
- **Operations:** Separates conjecture, finite certificate, generic theorem, and physical specialization; checks hypotheses claim by claim; records counterexamples or missing promotions; and updates the research map/ledger only after an audit gate closes.
- **Boundary:** These documents manage epistemic status. They do not independently create a proof merely by marking an item established.

Relevant artifacts include the emergence ledgers, audit reports, research log, strategic memo, and prior-art report.

#### Precision-flow proposal

- **Primitives:** A degree-two rounding cover `pi: X -> B`, a deck involution, observable and residual coordinates, and a proposed precision-flow tuple.
- **Operations:** Splits data into descended/invariant and sheet-dependent parts, tracks rounding residuals across the cover, and specifies what a later exact or typed numerical implementation would need to certify.
- **Boundary:** The PRED-006 entry is a design/proof-program artifact, not an implemented numerical kernel in this directory.

### `galois_horizon_cover_v1_0_publication_package/`

- **Primitives:** A monic quadratic horizon cover, four-charge quintic mass fiber, `S_5` monodromy, Kummer square-class modules, degree-20 and degree-40 decorated covers, tame inertia, and the branch-incidence scheme.
- **Core equations:** The Kerr-Newman pair is encoded by `z^2 - e1 z + e2 = 0`, with `e1 = 2 pi (2M^2-Q^2)` and `e2 = pi^2(4J^2+Q^4)`. The four-charge seed satisfies `4M = sum_i sqrt(m^2+N_i^2)`.
- **Operations:** Uses Vieta/Galois descent for the two-horizon dictionary; norm elimination and modular Frobenius factorization for the `S_5` obstruction; resultants for entropy minimality; divisorial valuations modulo 2 for Kummer rank; wreath-product lifting for normal closures; and Macaulay2 ideal operations for realized branch strata.
- **Certified conclusions recorded by the package:** Static and generic rotating ordered-horizon degree 20 with normal closure `C_2^2 wr S_5`; augmented axial-horizon degree 40 with closure `C_2^3 wr S_5`; prime/reduced/irreducible rotating difference ideal over the stated `QQ` incidence model.
- **Boundary:** The package itself fences generic `J` from the special fiber `J=0`, does not promote `QQ` irreducibility to arbitrary geometric irreducibility, and recommends specialist counter-audit/prior-art review.

The `.tex` file is the mathematical source, the PDF is its compiled presentation, `supporting_reports/` supplies proof dossiers, and `certificates/` supplies executable and raw computational evidence.

### `scripts/`

#### `abel_ruffini_black_holes.py`

- Builds the sign norm `N(u) = product_epsilon(4M - sum_i epsilon_i sqrt(u+a_i))` by repeated quadratic norm steps, specializes it, factors it modulo good primes, and reads factor-degree patterns as Frobenius cycle types.
- A transitive quintic witness containing a cycle type unavailable to the solvable transitive quintic groups certifies the `S_5` specialization and hence a generic non-solvability obstruction.

#### `allk_monodromy_certificates.py`

- Implements integer polynomial addition, subtraction, multiplication, scaling, quadratic norm steps, modular division/GCD/powers, square-free checks, and Frobenius cycle extraction without a CAS.
- Rebuilds an anchor quintic and then tests selected `k` values against their predicted degree and symmetric-group monodromy certificates.

#### `collision_field_read.py`

- Substitutes the Kerr-Newman extremal/collision relations into symbolic curvature data, factors algebraic expressions, and compares square-free discriminant content.
- Discriminates between a constant field such as `Q(sqrt(-3))` and the surface-dependent field generated by `sqrt(M^4-M^2 Q^2-J^2)`.

#### `entropy_sum_resolvent.py`

- Works in the quotient quintic field `Q[u]/(N(u))`, reconstructs signed radical representatives `g_i(u)`, and verifies `g_i^2 = u+N_i^2` and `sum g_i = 4M` modulo `N`.
- Forms the entropy-sum element `gamma(u)`, verifies the product collapse, computes `Res_u(N(u), X-gamma(u))`, and tests irreducibility/minimal degree to determine whether entropy inherits the mass-field obstruction.

#### `four_charge_crown.py`

- Manipulates hyperbolic boost variables and symmetric products for the Cvetic-Youm family, verifies Kerr and equal-boost Kerr-Newman anchors, derives the entropy product, and tracks the physical-field cover degree.
- Uses exact symbolic simplification/factorization; its conclusions depend on the encoded parametric family and validation anchors.

#### `kn_three_face_field.py`

- Differentiates the Kerr-Newman fundamental relation, constructs inverse-channel metric factors, and evaluates leading scalar-curvature coefficients at the extremal and two reflection-fixed faces.
- Factors radicands and minimal polynomials to identify whether each coefficient is rational or lives in a nontrivial quadratic extension of the physical function field.

## Artifact and evidence rules

- `.tex` and `.md` sources carry arguments, definitions, and scope statements.
- `.py` and `.m2` files carry executable calculations whose assumptions must be read with the code.
- `.out.txt` and result `.txt`/`.json` files record particular executions; they are not reusable algorithms.
- `.pdf` files are compiled presentations of source material, not independent proofs.
- `.zip` files are transport snapshots and may contain older or duplicate artifacts.
- Versioned research maps, logs, and ledgers are historical states. Later versions supersede status summaries but do not erase earlier derivations or audit trails.
