# Essential Dimension Collapse at AF Saturation

**Status:** OBSERVATION (single surface) — Tier 3
**Type:** Observed confluence of three framework concepts
**Source:** Session handoff 10 April 2026, Section 3.7

---

## Observation

On the isotropic surface F = x₁² + x₂² + x₃² + a(x₁x₂ + x₂x₃ + x₃x₁) − 3 at (1,1,1):

At a = 2:
- K_G = (a−2)²/(3c²) = 0
- Polynomial factors: F = (x₁ + x₂ + x₃)² − 3
- Essential dimension drops 3 → 1
- κ_c = κ_s = 4/(3c²) — perfect coupling-self balance
- Δ_AF still equals 0 — AF saturation maintained

Three framework concepts meet at one geometric event:
1. **Decomposition:** coupling-self balance (κ_c = κ_s)
2. **Essential dimension:** polynomial factors, dimension collapses
3. **AF saturation:** Δ_AF = 0 maintained through the transition

## Significance

This is a beautiful mathematical confluence but it's observed on ONE surface with maximal symmetry (S₃-symmetric). It could be a deep structural connection or it could be an artefact of the symmetry. Without evidence from additional surfaces (preferably with less symmetry), this is an invitation to investigate, not a result to claim.
