## 1. Scope and status discipline

This dossier begins the full ramification classification of the wreath covers

```text
C_2^2 wreath S_5                       static ordered horizon,
C_2^2 wreath S_5                       rotating ordered horizon,
C_2^3 wreath S_5                       augmented rotating cover.
```

It separates three phenomena which must not be conflated:

1. **permutation branch**, caused by ramification of the mass-quintic splitting cover;
2. **Kummer branch**, caused by odd valuations of the channel radicands; and
3. **rank-drop strata**, where specialized square classes acquire relations even though the parameter divisor need not be a branch divisor of the generic cover.

The classification below is complete on the standard generic affine open where

```text
2P product_{a<b}(N_a^2-N_b^2)
```

and the R9 regularity denominators are inverted. Section 12 then classifies the generic points of the natural excluded boundary divisors. The nonemptiness and incidence multiplicities of optional higher-codimension collision strata depend on a chosen compactified discriminant scheme; their inertia, whenever realized, is nevertheless completely determined by Sections 4, 5, and 10.

## 3. Divisorial Kummer inertia

Let `v` be a geometric discrete valuation of `E_J` with residue characteristic different from two. For radicands `r_{alpha,i}`, define the parity vector

```text
p_v=(v(r_{alpha,i}) mod 2)_{alpha,i}.                    (3.1)
```

### Theorem 3.1 — Kummer inertia vector

After henselizing at `v`:

```text
p_v=0
  => the multiquadratic extension is unramified at v;

p_v!=0
  => the inertia group is cyclic of order two,
     generated by the sign vector p_v.                  (3.2)
```

#### Proof

Write each radicand as

```text
r_{alpha,i}=pi^{m_{alpha,i}} epsilon_{alpha,i}
```

with `epsilon_{alpha,i}` a unit. Unit square-root extensions are unramified in residue characteristic different from two. All ramified quadratic characters therefore factor through the parity of the common uniformizer `pi`. There is at most one ramified quadratic extension, generated by `sqrt(pi)`, and its nontrivial inertia element flips exactly those square roots for which `m_{alpha,i}` is odd. This is (3.2). ∎

### Consequence 3.2

Several radicands may vanish oddly on the same prime, but the generic divisorial inertia is still one copy of `C_2`. The parity row specifies which signs that single involution flips.

## 4. Higher Kummer strata

Let `D_1,...,D_t` be distinct Kummer branch divisors meeting on a normal-crossing locus, with parity rows

```text
p_1,...,p_t.
```

### Proposition 4.1 — stratum inertia rank

The Kummer part of the local inertia group along their common stratum is

```text
I_Kum ~= (C_2)^r,
r=rank_F2{p_1,...,p_t}.                                  (4.1)
```

Thus branch-divisor counts do not determine the local inertia rank: linear dependence among parity vectors lowers it.

#### Proof

On a strict henselian normal-crossing chart, choose independent local parameters for the branch components. The tame local fundamental group is generated by commuting loops around these parameters. The Kummer character of the `a`th loop is `p_a`. Its image is therefore the span of the parity rows. ∎

## 5. Wreath-product conjugacy data

Let

```text
W_s=(C_2^s)^5 semidirect S_5.
```

An element is written `(a;sigma)`, where `a` is a five-sheet sign assignment. For each cycle `c` of `sigma`, define its channel sum

```text
kappa_c=sum_{i in c} a_i in C_2^s.                       (5.1)
```

The conjugacy class in the wreath product is determined by the cycle lengths together with these cycle sums. In particular:

- a sheet-local Kummer inertia element has `sigma=1` and one nonzero fixed-point label;
- a pure mass-discriminant inertia element has `sigma` a transposition and zero channel sum on that two-cycle;
- a transposition with nonzero two-cycle sum belongs to a different wreath conjugacy class and is not the generic mass-discriminant inertia established below.

This gives a coordinate-free label for every inertia class used in the classification.

### 5.1 Complete cyclic inertia catalogue

Every tame divisorial inertia group is cyclic. For an element `(a;sigma)`, a cycle of length `l` contributes order

```text
l       when its channel sum kappa_c is zero,
2l      when its channel sum kappa_c is nonzero.         (5.2)
```

The order of the full element is the least common multiple of the cycle contributions. For five sheets, the complete permutation catalogue is:

| Cycle partition of five | Possible inertia orders after coloring |
|---|---|
| `1+1+1+1+1` | `1,2` |
| `2+1+1+1` | `2,4` |
| `2+2+1` | `2,4` |
| `3+1+1` | `3,6` |
| `3+2` | `6,12` |
| `4+1` | `4,8` |
| `5` | `5,10` |

For each cycle, the actual nonzero value of `kappa_c` records the channel type. There are three possible nonzero colors in a two-channel cover and seven in the augmented three-channel cover. Consequently, cycle partition plus channel sum on every cycle is a complete classification of all possible tame divisorial inertia conjugacy classes in these wreath groups.

## 6. Generic branch divisors

### 6.1 Mass discriminant

Let

```text
B_Delta : Delta_mass=0.
```

At a generic smooth point of `B_Delta`, exactly two mass roots collide and all Kummer radicands are regular and nonzero after excluding their own branch loci. The splitting-cover inertia is a transposition.

Because the Kummer layer is unramified there, the total generic inertia is conjugate to

```text
T=(0;(ij)),                                               (6.1)
```

a pure transposition of order two. This applies to all three covers.

**Status:** proved on the open part of `B_Delta` disjoint from Kummer branch divisors. The collision intersections are classified group-theoretically in Section 9 and still require local transversality checks in the concrete mass family.

### 6.2 Signed contact walls

For a sign vector `epsilon in {+1,-1}^4`, define

```text
ell_epsilon=4M-sum_a epsilon_a N_a,
s_epsilon=product_a epsilon_a.                          (6.2)
```

There are eight even walls (`s_epsilon=+1`) and eight odd walls (`s_epsilon=-1`). On the distinguished mass sheet above a generic contact wall, the R9 incidence calculation gives

```text
even contact: v(u_i,gamma_i)=(1,0) mod 2,
odd contact:  v(u_i,gamma_i)=(1,1) mod 2.                (6.3)
```

The coefficient `-16J^2` makes every `delta_j` a Gauss unit at these wall valuations, so the augmented rotating inertia types are

```text
C_i^+ : A_i,                                             (6.4)
C_i^- : A_i+G_i.                                         (6.5)
```

For the rotating ordered-horizon cover:

```text
C_i^+ : unbranched,
C_i^- : G_i.                                             (6.6)
```

For the static cover, using channel order `(u_i,gamma_i)`:

```text
C_i^+ : (1,0)_i,
C_i^- : (1,1)_i.                                         (6.7)
```

The sixteen wall equations are not components of the mass discriminant. The exact all-plus witness in R9 proves this for one wall, and independent sign changes `N_a -> +/-N_a` preserve the mass polynomial while carrying the all-plus wall to every signed wall.

### 6.2.1 No hidden odd `u`--`gamma` branch on the affine open

On the incidence normalization, the zeros of `u_i` on the localized generic affine model are precisely the signed contact components. At any other height-one prime, use

```text
gamma_i(gamma_i-4P)=4u_i beta_i^2.                       (6.8)
```

Since `P` is a unit, `gamma_i` and `gamma_i-4P` cannot both have positive valuation. Taking valuations in (6.8) shows

```text
v(gamma_i)+v(gamma_i-4P)
  = v(u_i)+2v(beta_i).                                   (6.9)
```

Therefore an odd valuation of `gamma_i` forces an odd valuation of `u_i`; if `u_i` is a unit, every positive valuation of `gamma_i` is even. Hence the even/odd contact types exhaust the affine Kummer branch contributed by the two old channels. Zeros of `beta_i` away from contacts can change multiplicities but cannot create a new odd parity row.

### 6.3 Rotating difference divisors

For each sheet, let

```text
Z_i=V(delta_i).
```

Each irreducible component of `Z_i` occurs with multiplicity one because `delta_i` is squarefree in `J`. No component is shared between `Z_i` and `Z_j`, since

```text
delta_i-delta_j=gamma_i-gamma_j in E^*,
i!=j.                                                    (6.10)
```

Every component of `Z_i` has inertia

```text
D_i                                                     (6.11)
```

in both rotating covers. The polynomial `delta_i` may be irreducible or may split into two components over `E`; the inertia label is the same in either case.

On the normal-crossing part of the rotating branch locus, Proposition 4.1 gives the following local groups.

### 8.1 Ordered-horizon cover

```text
C_i^- intersect Z_j:
  <G_i,D_j> ~= C_2^2                     for all i,j.    (8.1)
```

The vectors remain independent even when `i=j` because they occupy different channel coordinates.

### 8.2 Augmented rotating cover

```text
C_i^+ intersect Z_j:
  <A_i,D_j> ~= C_2^2,                                  (8.2)

  <A_i+G_i,D_j> ~= C_2^2.                              (8.3)
```

Even and odd contact components represent different normalized sign branches at `u=0`; their apparent intersections in the observable base must be resolved on the incidence normalization before Proposition 4.1 is applied.

## 9. Mixed permutation--Kummer strata

Let `T=(ij)` be the transposition inertia at a simple mass collision, and let `b_k` be a Kummer sign vector of channel type `b` supported on sheet `k`.

The local subgroup generated by `T` and `b_k` has two group-theoretic forms:

### 9.1 The marked sheet is fixed

If `k` is not in `{i,j}`, then `T` commutes with `b_k`, and

```text
<T,b_k> ~= C_2 x C_2.                                   (9.1)
```

### 9.2 The marked sheet collides

If `k=i`, then

```text
T b_i T^{-1}=b_j.
```

Hence

```text
<T,b_i>
  = <b_i,b_j> semidirect <T>
  ~= (C_2)^2 semidirect C_2
  ~= D_8
  ~= C_2 wreath S_2.                                    (9.2)
```

This is the smallest mixed wreath stratum: the collision duplicates a sheet-local Kummer character across the two-sheet orbit.

### Status fence

Equations (9.1)--(9.2) classify the subgroup forced by the wreath action **if** the corresponding branch components meet transversely in the concrete normalized model. They do not assert that every combinatorially possible intersection is nonempty. Establishing the realized mixed-stratum incidence scheme is a remaining geometric task.

## 10. Higher mass-collision strata

For a separable degree-five cover, the generic mass-discriminant stratum has transposition inertia. At higher collision loci, the permutation part is generated by the local collision graph:

- two disjoint simple collisions give a subgroup `C_2 x C_2` generated by disjoint transpositions;
- a versal triple collision gives `S_3`;
- a collision partition with blocks of sizes `m_1,...,m_t` is contained in the corresponding Young subgroup

  ```text
  S_{m_1} x ... x S_{m_t} subset S_5.                  (10.1)
  ```

The full local group is obtained by adjoining the orbit of every Kummer vector present on the stratum. Algebraically, if `P` is the local permutation subgroup and `U` is the span of the `P`-orbits of the incident parity vectors, then

```text
I_local subseteq U semidirect P.                         (10.2)
```

Equality holds when the local loops realize the indicated permutation generators and Kummer rows independently. The concrete transversality and realization of these higher collision types remain to be proved; (10.2) is the controlling classification theorem, not an existence assertion.

## 11. The `J=0` rank-drop stratum is not a branch divisor

At the `J`-adic valuation,

```text
v_J(u_i)=v_J(gamma_i)=v_J(delta_i)=0                    (11.1)
```

because

```text
delta_i(0)=gamma_i-4P != 0.
```

Therefore the generic rotating Kummer cover is unramified along `J=0`.

Nevertheless, specialization gives

```text
[delta_i(0)]=[u_i][gamma_i],                             (11.2)
```

so the square-class rank drops after passing to the fiber. Thus:

```text
J=0 is a Kummer relation divisor,
not a generic ramification divisor.                      (11.3)
```

This distinction explains how the augmented group can drop from `C_2^3 wreath S_5` to the static `C_2^2 wreath S_5` on selected components without a nontrivial generic inertia element at `J=0`.

### 11.1 Rotation infinity

Put `t=1/J` at infinity on the projective `J`-line. Then

```text
delta_i
  = t^(-2)(-16+(gamma_i-4P)t^2).                         (11.4)
```

Thus

```text
v_t(delta_i)=-2,
v_t(u_i)=v_t(gamma_i)=0.                                 (11.5)
```

All parities are even, so `J=infinity` is unramified in both rotating Kummer covers. A possible nonsquare residue of `-16` can create an unramified constant residue extension, but not inertia.

## 12. Degenerate and compactification boundaries

### 12.1 A single vanishing charge: `P=0`

Consider the generic point of `N_a=0`, with the other charges nonzero and away from the signed contact walls. The wall-norm formula shows that every `u_i` is a unit there. The entropy identity specializes to

```text
gamma_i^2=4u_i beta_i^2.                                (12.1)
```

At the generic point `beta_i` and `gamma_i` are nonzero, so

```text
u_i=(gamma_i/(2beta_i))^2.                               (12.2)
```

Thus the axial class `[u_i]` becomes trivial in the specialized sheet field. All three displayed radicands have valuation zero along `N_a=0`, so the divisor is generically unramified. It is instead a Kummer relation divisor:

```text
static rank per sheet:       2 -> 1,
augmented rotating rank:     3 -> at most 2,
ordered rotating rank:       not forced to drop.         (12.3)
```

Further charge-zero intersections are obtained by repeating this specialization and recomputing the residual nonzero channel classes; they introduce no new divisorial inertia rule.

### 12.2 Equal squared charges: `N_a^2=N_b^2`

This divisor belongs to the polynomial discriminant but is generically a crossing of normalized sheets, not a ramification divisor of the normalization.

To see this, write locally

```text
N_a^2=A+t,
N_b^2=A-t.
```

The two radical branches differing by interchange of the `a` and `b` signs contain

```text
sqrt(u+A+t)-sqrt(u+A-t)
  = t/sqrt(u+A)+O(t^3),                                  (12.4)
```

with the opposite branch carrying the negative expression. Away from higher critical loci, the implicit-function theorem gives two normalized mass roots

```text
u_+(t)=u_0+ct+O(t^2),
u_-(t)=u_0-ct+O(t^2),
c!=0.                                                    (12.5)
```

They cross at `t=0` but are individually regular in `t`. Hence the local normalization is unramified and its inertia is trivial. The vanishing of the polynomial discriminant has even crossing multiplicity, exactly as for `(X-t)(X+t)`.

At the generic charge-collision point the Kummer radicands are units, so there is no additional sign inertia. The specialized polynomial model loses sheet separation, and conjugate `gamma` values may coincide, but this is a coalescence stratum rather than branch monodromy.

### 12.3 Reciprocal sub-balance: colored order-four inertia

Fix a contact sign vector and put

```text
n_a=epsilon_a N_a,
S_1=sum_a 1/n_a,
S_3=sum_a 1/n_a^3,
ell=4M-sum_a n_a.                                       (12.6)
```

The incidence expansion is

```text
ell
  = (S_1/2)u-(S_3/8)u^2+O(u^3).                         (12.7)
```

On the generic reciprocal sub-balance stratum

```text
ell=0,
S_1=0,
S_3!=0,                                                 (12.8)
```

equation (12.7) has local form

```text
ell=unit*u^2.                                            (12.9)
```

Thus the mass-sheet part has transposition monodromy. Moreover, with

```text
beta=e_3(w)+u e_1(w),
```

one obtains

```text
beta
  = B_1 u+O(u^2),
B_1=4M-(s_epsilon P/2)S_3,                              (12.10)
```

which is nonzero generically. The entropy identity then gives

```text
s_epsilon=+1:
  v_u(gamma)=0,
  v_u(gamma-4P)=3;

s_epsilon=-1:
  v_u(gamma)=3,
  v_u(gamma-4P)=0.                                      (12.11)
```

For generic `J`, every `delta_i` remains a unit. The resulting local inertia is:

| Cover | Even sub-balance | Odd sub-balance |
|---|---|---|
| static two-channel | `C_4`, transposition colored `(1,0)` | `C_4`, transposition colored `(1,1)` |
| rotating ordered horizon | `C_2`, pure transposition | `C_4`, transposition colored `G` |
| augmented rotation | `C_4`, transposition colored `A` | `C_4`, transposition colored `A+G` |

Indeed, adjoining `x=sqrt(u)` to (12.9) gives `ell=unit*x^4`. A generator sends one colliding mass sheet to the other and has nonzero channel sum on the two-cycle, so it has order four by (5.2). This is the non-transverse alternative to the transverse `D_8` subgroup of Section 9.

If `S_3` or `B_1` also vanishes, one has entered a higher-contact stratum; its inertia is obtained by continuing the displayed Taylor expansion and applying the colored-cycle rule.

### 12.4 The affine leading-coefficient divisor `M=0`

The mass norm has leading coefficient `-2^24 M^6`, so the monic affine `u`-chart degenerates at `M=0`. The incidence cover itself does not.

For a balanced sign pattern, the large-`u` expansion has its leading `sqrt(u)` term cancelled. Solving the next term against `4M` gives

```text
u_i=c_i M^(-2)+O(1)                                    (12.12)
```

for three generic projective mass sheets, with distinct nonzero `c_i`; the remaining sheets stay finite after changing to the reciprocal coordinate. Hence every projective mass branch is Laurent in an integral power of `M`, and there is no permutation inertia around `M=0`.

On the balanced sheets, the `u^2` terms in

```text
gamma_i=2(e_4(w)+u_i e_2(w)+u_i^2+P)
```

cancel, leaving an integral power of `u_i`. Consequently the pole orders of `u_i`, `gamma_i`, and `delta_i` are even. The generic point of `M=0` is therefore unramified after projective normalization. It is a chart boundary, not a branch divisor.

### 12.5 Generic weighted infinity

The equations are homogeneous for the weights

```text
wt(M)=wt(N_a)=1,
wt(J)=wt(u_i)=2,
wt(gamma_i)=wt(delta_i)=4.                              (12.13)
```

Along the generic radial infinity divisor, the corresponding pole orders are

```text
v(u_i)=-2,
v(gamma_i)=v(delta_i)=-4.                               (12.14)
```

They are all even. Homogeneity also makes the normalized mass sheets constant in the radial coordinate, so there is no permutation inertia. Generic weighted infinity is unramified. Special subvarieties of the infinity divisor are governed by their leading-form collision partition and the colored-partition catalogue of Section 5.1.

## 13. Complete local classification theorem

### Theorem 13.1

For the static, rotating ordered-horizon, and augmented rotating wreath covers, every tame local inertia group is determined as follows.

1. At a height-one Kummer divisor above the unramified mass locus, inertia is the cyclic group generated by its parity row.
2. At a transverse intersection of Kummer divisors, the Kummer inertia subgroup is the `F_2`-span of their parity rows.
3. At a mass-collision stratum with local permutation group `P`, let `U` be the `P`-stable span of all incident Kummer parity rows. The local group embeds in `U semidirect P` and equals the generated subgroup.
4. Divisorial cyclic conjugacy classes are classified completely by the mass-cycle partition and the channel sum on each cycle.
5. For the named generic and boundary strata, the realized groups are exactly those recorded in Sections 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12.

#### Proof

Items 1 and 2 are Theorem 3.1 and Proposition 4.1. Conjugating a parity row by a local permutation generator supplies its complete `P`-orbit, proving item 3. The standard conjugacy theorem for a wreath product with abelian base identifies conjugacy classes by cycle lengths and cycle sums, and equation (5.2) gives their orders, proving item 4. The local calculations in the cited sections prove item 5. ∎

## 14. Realization versus classification

The theorem classifies every possible inertia class and computes every named codimension-one boundary in the present family. A separate discriminant-incidence computation may still ask which optional higher-codimension colored partitions are nonempty, how many irreducible components they have, and which containment relations occur between them.

That is a realization-poset problem, not an unresolved inertia problem: once a stratum's collision partition and incident parity rows are supplied, Theorem 13.1 returns its local group without further Galois calculation.
