## Verdict

The revision ledger's central doctrine is correct: a release floor, refusal,
or theorem nonclaim is not a ban on investigation. Three qualifications are
required:

1. `REFUTED` must be a first-class expansion outcome.
2. PF-0 plan validity never replaces executor proof obligations and imports no
   authority outside its exact source-locked domain.
3. Correct theorem nonclaims should not be duplicated with expansion boilerplate
   in every paper. A central authority record plus corrected operative handoffs
   is clearer and avoids documentation bloat.

The ledger was written without disk access. Its section titled “Files revised”
was a proposed edit map; those edits were not present in the named files. This
is provenance, not a defect in the mathematical diagnosis.

The inventory also contained two locator mismatches: the v0.1 CCE handoff is
not present, and the live v0.2 handoff is under
`research/paper/Theorems/DBP/technical_supplements/`, not `upload/`.

## Corrections to avoid new doctrine

### Do not amend every theorem nonclaim

Statements such as “this theorem does not prove whole-surface primitivity” are
mathematically useful and should remain. They delimit the proof. The artificial
restriction arose only when later handoffs interpreted that sentence as “do
not investigate whole-surface primitivity.” The correct fix is to amend the
operative workflow/handoff and expansion ledger, not to pad every theorem with
the same motivational paragraph.

### Do not make expansion queues infinite

An identified expansion must receive a concrete investigation and one of four
outcomes:

```text
PROMOTED
ACTIVE_PROOF
BLOCKED(named obligation)
REFUTED(proof or counterexample)
```

`ACTIVE_PROOF` is not permission to leave vague “future work.” It must name the
next exact object. Conversely, a proof of impossibility terminates that version
of the claim and redirects work to a reduced target.

### Do not broaden PF-0 by analogy

The current PF-0 confirmation exhaustively covers six requests only. CCE-4's
new arbitrary-precision wrapper does not retroactively enlarge that Pathfinder
theorem; it uses the fixed route/class proof and independently scales the
evaluation schedule. Any wider Pathfinder provider needs its own admitted
domain audit and source closure.
