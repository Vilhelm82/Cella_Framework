## Ranked directions worth exploring

### 1. The `kappa_{r;p,q}` grid at `n >= 4`

This is the highest-upside theorem candidate.

It is the cleanest “first original mathematics” bet because the edges are now mapped but the interior is not. The record says:

```text
pure-coupling column        mapped via the depth theorem
r = 2 row                   mapped via the triangle / shadow law
parity boundary             mapped
mixed-bidegree interior     unmapped
minimal coupling degree p   open
```

The goal is not to compute more examples for their own sake. The goal is to extract a theorem:

```text
recurrence law
forced-vanishing law
signature law
minimal-depth law
```

A good first campaign would preregister exact extraction for:

```text
n = 4, 5
r = 2, 3
```

Then look for a conjecture strong enough to be wrong.

Candidate conjectures worth trying to kill:

```text
1. Pascal-type coefficient recurrence in (p, q)
2. Lorentzian-to-higher-signature law generalizing the n=3 (2,1) coupling form
3. Specht-depth threshold: which mixed bidegrees see which representation depth
4. Vanishing theorem for low p below a depth threshold
```

This is the most “mathematics first, engine second” direction.

If this works, role non-solvability becomes a diagnostic object, not a computational exception.

The goal should be either an injectivity theorem or a counterexample theorem:

### 6. Codimension `>= 2` constraint systems

This is not in the original three dark corners, but the lever audit ranks it as the top “engine soon” R&D priority.

The theory may change when the object is not:



The dark corner is interaction between constraints:


Any non-commutation there is a serious discovery candidate.
