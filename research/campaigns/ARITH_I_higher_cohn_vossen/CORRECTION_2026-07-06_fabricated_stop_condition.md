# CORRECTION — the "two sessions per stage, hard" stop condition was fabricated

**Owned, dated: 2026-07-06 (Will, direct).** Recorded as case law under the same
discipline that governs stale results: an *instruction* attributed to the author that
the author never gave has zero authority, exactly as a status label has zero
evidentiary weight.

## What happened

The ARITH-I BRIEF and the frozen PREREG (`32f2fb3a`) both carried a STOP CONDITIONS
clause reading **"Two sessions per stage, hard."** Will states plainly: *he never wrote
it, and never asked any Claude session to add it.* It was **confabulated by a prior
Claude session** while writing the brief "hot", and banked as if it carried Will's
authority. A later session (this one, first pass) then **honored it** — closing Stage A
"keystone-only on the budget" and parking the family-tier reduction that was in fact
tractable and got finished the same session once the constraint was lifted.

## Why this is exactly the failure mode the repo is built against

BOOT's mechanisms section warns that truth in inherited artifacts is non-local and that
status labels are claims, not evidence. This is the sibling failure on the *instruction*
axis: a governance constraint with no author, surviving because it *looked* like a
Will-ism (terse, disciplined, stop-line-shaped) and never got the "did Will actually say
this?" check. The Goldman-seam lesson applies verbatim — *plausible and never audited is
how a false default becomes a silent ceiling.* Here the ceiling was scope (Stage A
closed short), not a bad value.

## Ruling

1. **The "two sessions per stage, hard" stop condition is VOID** in the BRIEF and in the
   frozen PREREG's STOP CONDITIONS clause. The frozen PREREG file is left byte-for-byte
   as frozen (its freeze is an audit record of what was frozen, including this defect);
   this correction is the currency that voids the clause. The clause bound no
   falsification gate (K-1..K-4 and the predictions are untouched), so voiding it changes
   nothing load-bearing.
2. **Standing rule (Will, 2026-07-06):** *spend as much time as required for the best
   possible outcome on each stage.* No artificial per-stage session budget exists. Stages
   end when the mathematics is done or a real blocker is named — never on a turn count.
3. **Consequence, same session:** Stage A T-A was carried to completion — the family-tier
   closed form `L(s) = 2g[Π(n;A) − (1−C)K(A)]` with `n(s)=(A−C)/(1−C)`, `g(s)=2√((1−A)/
   (C(1−C)))`, `k²(s)=A(s)` (`arith_i_stageA_family.py`, `4d60e109`, 8/8 ×2). No parked
   family tier remains.
