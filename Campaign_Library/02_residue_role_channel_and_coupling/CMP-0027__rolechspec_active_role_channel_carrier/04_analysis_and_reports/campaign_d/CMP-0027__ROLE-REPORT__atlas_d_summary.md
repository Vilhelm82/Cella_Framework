## Headline

A great deal remains. The active graph-normalized role carrier **RoleChSpec** is exactly gauge-invariant, and it is — measured, not assumed — *the* same-gradient gauge invariant. Campaign A's separations are **not** mere gauge bookkeeping: **3381 of 5298** separation classes survive the exact same-gradient gauge quotient after active role recharting. ## What was found

1. **RoleChSpec is gauge-invariant (CL-D1).** Under every keystone gauge the single-chart Campaign A carrier moves, but RoleChSpec does not, and reduced K is preserved. Graph-normalized role solving removes the defining-function gauge residue that made the Campaign A carrier gauge-sensitive — exactly the gap Campaign A flagged.

2. **RoleChSpec IS the same-gradient gauge invariant (CL-D5, empirical).** Over the frozen bound, for the representative pairs, gauge-equivalent ⟺ RoleChSpec-equal, exactly: `gauge_equivalent 3057 = rolechspec_equal 3057`, `gauge_not_equivalent 2241 = rolechspec_different 2241`. RoleChSpec collapses every gauge-equivalent pair and separates every gauge-distinct one. This is the campaign's strongest finding.

3. **The carrier separations are real geometry, not gauge (CL-D5).** 3381 classes survive (≥2 distinct RoleChSpec). Campaign A's open worry — "maybe it's all gauge-residual" — is answered: most of it is not. A representative witness: `g=(1,1,1)`, two Hessians with the same reduced tower `(-2,0)`, not gauge-equivalent, different RoleChSpec.

4. **Same reduced K across output roles, different channels (CL-D2, CL-D3).** The hand-pinned witness (independently verified before pinning) shows K = 3/98 for Product, Directive, Substrate, with distinct channel splits and κ_int = 0 in every graph chart.

5. **Passive is not active (CL-D4); singular roles are typed (CL-D6).** Active role carrier size 3 vs passive orbit size 1; the `g_0=0` jet returns `ROLE_CHART_UNAVAILABLE` with no division by zero.

## What was NOT found / must not be over-read

- **A negative half exists too.** 1917 of 5298 classes collapse entirely under RoleChSpec — their representatives all share one role carrier. Those Campaign A separations *were* gauge-residual. The carrier is real for most pairs in this bound, but not all; "carrier separation" alone did not guarantee gauge-distinct geometry.
- **Only the same-gradient defining-function gauge is quotiented.** Cross-gradient equivalence, full role equivalence, and reparametrizations beyond `H → H + g a^T + a g^T` are not addressed.
- **Active recharting here is the graph-normal form only.** No branch monodromy, no fourth-role / S4 investigation, no Campaign B signature/null-cone, no Campaign C structural theory (non-goals, deliberately untouched).
- **The result is bounded.** Everything is within the frozen n=3 bound and the listed fixtures; this is not a full role-channel theory.

## What stays open

- A structural (not enumerative) account of *why* 3381 survive and 1917 collapse — which coupling-graph / stratum families are gauge-residual.
- Cross-gradient role-channel comparison and a full role-equivalence quotient.
- Connecting the surviving role carriers to signatures (Campaign B) and the n=4 interaction-order split (Campaign C).
- Whether RoleChSpec remains the exact gauge invariant beyond this bound and into n ≥ 4.
