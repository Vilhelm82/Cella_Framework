## Conversation 1 — TRIAL-law standing decision

**The law (Facet C GO, strikeable):** every grader embeds its
governing prereg clause VERBATIM in runner source; the gate
string-compares each embedded clause against the frozen prereg at
runtime and refuses on drift.

**Evidence:**
- Origin: two grader-drift defects in the two facets before the law —
  PA.4 and PB.4, both "grader narrower than frozen text," both caught
  at run 1, chains preserved.
- Since activation (Facets C, D, E, F — four facets, eight battery
  executions, every test run): **zero grader-drift defects.**
- Amendment #2 exercised the law's intended workflow live: the moment
  PC.2's prereg text changed, the gate would have refused execution
  until GRADER_CLAUSES matched — parity was forced, not remembered.
- Chat-side verification used the law every checkpoint: byte-comparing
  clauses became a mechanical step instead of a judgement call.
- Cost: one verbatim clause copy per grader per facet (two files must
  move together). In practice the cost surfaced once (amendment #2)
  and the law itself turned it into a forced, same-commit step.

**Scribe recommendation (inference, high confidence):** adopt as
standing law for graded campaigns — every grader that enforces a
frozen prediction embeds its clause verbatim; the gate
string-compares at runtime. The mechanism is cheap, was exercised in
anger once, and the before/after tally (2 → 0) is the cleanest
discipline signal this campaign produced. **Decision: Will's.**
